streetbeest
a production of sprout & co.

The streetbeest is an experiment in expanding the educational capacity of artistic work and public spectacle.

The project began at sprout's studios as part of the 2010 HONK! Festival in Somerville, Massachussets.

This site is the home of the streetbeest. Read more about the project, and then sign up for the mailing list, and follow @streetbeest. If you're interested in lending a hand, let us know.

about the ’beest

The first incarnation of the streetbeest was a 13′ tall mechanical sculpture of wood and PVC. 17′ wide, the streetbeest occupied two, full lanes as it paraded down Massachusetts Avenue in the 2010 HONK! Festival. The streetbeest was built by more than four dozen volunteers of a wide variety of backgrounds—some artists and some engineers, but most people involved had little or no building experience.

After the streetbeest's succesful debut, we're working to expand the scope of the project to sharpen the focus on what we feel are its most exciting lessons:

  • Artistic work & public spectacle offer rich learning and teaching opportunities embedded in authentic community organizing. Unfortunately, these opportunities often go unrecognized and unexploited.
  • Art doesn't have to be simply for consumption—it can be an active collaboration between an idea, artist(s), and an audience: people can engage with the art by creating their own and riffing off the original work.

We're developing a series of workshops, documentation, and seminars which explore the design and construction of mechanisms and kinetic sculpture as a context for diving into public spectacle, structural design, engineering, and geometry. If you'd like to get involved—with teaching, graphic design, engineering, grantwriting, or in some other way—let us know!

about sprout

sprout is a community education and research organization devoted to creating and supporting the community-driven learning, teaching, and investigation of science. We're united by a passion to reclaim science as a richly personal and creative craft. Through our programs, stories, & studios we're working to make our vision real in Somerville.

You might say we're working to create a community college that lives up to its name—not a college in a community or a school in a building, but a community of people who work together as colleagues to explore questions they care about.
